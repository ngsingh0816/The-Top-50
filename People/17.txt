Alexander Fleming
Alexander Fleming is the scientist who is noted for developing Penicillin, a type of antibiotics that form from fungi, which greatly helped in saving people's lives as well as giving quick and speedy recoveries. Without Penicillin, general antibiotics would not be available today and without those, many people would die from infections. Alexander Fleming is influential because he developed an antidote that cured a wide range of medical problems.
