John Locke
John Locke was a philosopher who is regarded as the father of liberalism, a philosophy with a limited government with many rights for the individuals including freedom of speech, religion, press, assembly, and free market. Locke also was one of the first to be in the enlightenment, an age in the 18th century where reason was the main power. He inspired much of America's government and the government of other nations too. Overall, John Locke is influential because he created a social philosophy, which is still heavily in use today.
